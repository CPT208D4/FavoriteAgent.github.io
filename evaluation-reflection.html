Usability Testing

Survey and interview feedback after high-fidelity prototype

  • After completing the high-fidelity prototype, we conducted a survey and short interviews with volunteers.
  • The results showed that the front page was the most confusing part, they reported difficulties in understanding how to use it and only one participant was aware of the function of this system after the first seen of this system.
  • Some users also criticized the design of the front page and the report page, and some did not notice that the favorites collection could be expanded without guidance. One of the users I interviewed told me that you should add more messages informing users about the ai function, otherwise they might overlook it.
  • In addition, another user informs me that the ai chatting is not smart enough, which might stick to the favorite knowledge without considering the meaning of the question. Also, an active question could be added at the end of each response, which connects and binds more information in the favorites. None of the participants selected the weekly report as the most valuable feature, suggesting that this function may need to be redesigned in later iterations.
  • Despite these drawbacks, most participants still expressed a willingness to use the system and recommend it to others
