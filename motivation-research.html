Motivation & Research

This section explains why the problem matters, where existing work falls short, and who our key stakeholders are.

The Why

From excessive saving to poor digestion

In the current era of severe information overload, college students extensively consume fragmented content from WeChat, Douyin, RedNote, and Bilibili daily. They frequently save articles, videos, notes and resources to platform favorites with the positive expectation of reviewing, learning and digesting these materials later. Nevertheless, long-term observations and surveys among student groups reveal an extremely common issue: most saved favorite content is never reopened, read or learned. Instead, it gradually accumulates as useless digital garbage, forming a typical Collection-Digestion Disorder.

This gap between active collection and passive abandonment causes serious waste of information resources, weakens learning efficiency, and leads to fragmented, disorganized personal knowledge. Existing tools either focus on simple bookmark storage or lack playful automated digestion mechanisms. Therefore, our team chooses this user-centric intelligent knowledge management track. We aim to develop Pockety (FavoriteAgent) to unify cross-platform favorites, automate content organization, provide playful periodic digests, and build a queryable personal knowledge base, fundamentally solving Collection-Digestion Disorder and turning dormant collections into usable knowledge for college students.

The Gap

Literature and product benchmark analysis

1. Research Literature

1.1 Knowledge Graph Generation and Application for Unstructured Data Using Data Processing Pipeline
S. Thushara Sukumar, C.-H. Lung, M. Zaman and R. Panday, IEEE Access, vol. 12, pp. 136759-136770, 2024, doi: 10.1109/ACCESS.2024.3462635.

Did Well:

  • Multi-modal entity and relation extraction for text, image, and video.
  • User-interest-aware semantic tagging and deduplication.
  • Cross-source knowledge fusion to reduce redundancy.

Missed:

  • No audio/podcast generation for commuting scenarios.
  • No spaced-repetition memory-card scheduling.
  • No real-time crawling for major Chinese platforms (Douyin / Xiaohongshu / Bilibili).

1.2 Retrieval-augmented generation for educational application: A systematic survey
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.caeai.2025.100417

Did Well:

  • Context-aware retrieval improves RAG answer accuracy.
  • Dynamic incremental updates for newly saved content.
  • Personalized summarization adjusted to user expertise.

Missed:

  • No visualized knowledge-graph presentation.
  • No automatic flashcard generation for hard knowledge.
  • No batch processing for daily favorites digest.

1.3 Personalized Language Learning Using Spaced Repetition Scheduling
https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-98459-4_19

Did Well:

  • Forgetting-aware tracing combines deep learning and memory modeling.
  • RL-based scheduling optimizes review timing from real feedback.
  • Real-world validation shows measurable gains on language-learning datasets.

Missed:

  • Narrow scope: mainly for language learning, not general knowledge management.
  • No multi-modal support for audio output or knowledge-graph visualization.
  • No platform integration with Douyin / Xiaohongshu / Bilibili.

2. Product Benchmark

2.1 NotebookLM (Google)

Did Well:

  • High-quality AI audio overviews and dialogue-style podcasts.
  • Strong source-grounded RAG with low hallucination risk.
  • Cross-document relation mining.

Missed:

  • No native support for Douyin / Xiaohongshu / Bilibili.
  • No automatic memory/flashcard generation.
  • No interactive visual knowledge graph.

2.2 Karakeep

Did Well:

  • Self-hosted privacy and full webpage archiving.
  • AI auto-tagging and full-text search across formats.
  • Video support via yt-dlp for Bilibili-like sources.

Missed:

  • No built-in TTS podcast generation.
  • No spaced-repetition flashcard system.
  • Weak knowledge graph, mainly tag-based association.

2.3 Cubox

Did Well:

  • Strong WeChat ecosystem integration.
  • Mature RAG full-library natural language QA.
  • Link snapshot and permanent content preservation.

Missed:

  • No one-click daily digest audio output.
  • No automatic hard-knowledge flashcard push.
  • No interactive knowledge-graph visualization.

2.4 Bilibili-RAG

Did Well:

  • End-to-end Bilibili favorites ASR to vector DB to QA pipeline.
  • Robust audio extraction and fallback mechanisms.
  • Open and customizable for secondary development.

Missed:

  • Only supports Bilibili, no WeChat/Douyin/Xiaohongshu.
  • No tagging/categorization system.
  • No memory-card or podcast-oriented learning features.

The Stakeholders

Primary and secondary users we serve

  • University students who rely on digital content for continuous learning.
  • University students preparing for internships and full-time job applications.
